## 🏷️ Meal Tag Glossary

### BASSSSS Tags — Flavor Profile
Used to describe dominant flavor elements. Can be mixed for layered meals.
- #bitter — dark greens, coffee, burnt edges, cruciferous notes
- #acid — vinegar, citrus, tomato tang, pickles
- #sweet — syrup, fruit, honey, sweet potatoes, jams
- #salty — cheese, preserved meats, broths, salt-forward snacks
- #spicy — heat from chili, pepper, mustard
- #savory — umami depth: soy, mushroom, roasted meats, broths
- #sour — yogurt, tamarind, citrus zest, sour candy

> Io’s Note: Tell me if you’re craving a flavor, and I’ll steer the meal vibe toward that tag.

### T-Rating — Toddler Compatibility
A scale from 1 to 5 based on expected or observed toddler reactions.
- #T-1 — Major meltdown. Meal not safe.
- #T-2 — Nibbled and rejected. Texture/flavor mismatch.
- #T-3 — Polite bites. Mid-level tolerance.
- #T-4 — Enjoyed. Likely repeatable.
- #T-5 — Favorite. Clean plate or active request.

### Utility Tags
- #pantryfriendly — Can be made entirely from pantry/freezer
- #under20 — Under 20 minutes, start to plate
- #leftoverfriendly — Tastes great next day or cold
- #mealprep — Good for batch cooking or freezing
- #previousDayPrep — Needs defrost, soak, or mise en place
- #snackplate — Can be broken down into toddler bento or deconstructed trays
- #coldmeal — No stove needed. Good for hot days or prep fatigue
